Causes of Climate Change

Greenhouse Gases

One of the primary causes of climate change is the increased concentration of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere.

These gases, such as carbon dioxide, methane, and water vapor, trap heat from the sun and cause the Earth's temperature to rise.

The burning of fossil fuels, deforestation, and agriculture are major sources of greenhouse gas emissions.

Deforestation

Deforestation, or the cutting down of forests, is another major contributor to climate change.

Trees absorb carbon dioxide from the atmosphere, so when they are cut down, the carbon they store is released into the air.

Deforestation also reduces the number of trees available to absorb carbon dioxide, creating a cycle of even more emissions.

Transportation

The transportation sector is a significant contributor to climate change, as vehicles emit greenhouse gases during their operation.

Cars and trucks are the largest source of transportation emissions, but airplanes, ships, and trains also contribute to the problem.

Burning Fossil Fuels

Burning fossil fuels for energy is a major contributor to greenhouse gas emissions, and is a significant cause of climate change.

Agriculture

Agriculture, particularly livestock farming, generates large amounts of methane, which is a potent greenhouse gas and contributes significantly to climate change.
